Our History

Sare Silver Springs School is a leading center of learning and excellence located in Omboga Zone, Rachuonyo North Sub-County, just 1.2 km from Wikondiek Stadium, Homa Bay County. Since our founding in 2014, we have remained true to our vision of offering top-quality education to the children of Kanayaluo and beyond. What began as a modest initiative has grown into one of the most respected schools in the region, producing learners who excel not only academically but also in discipline, talent, and character. At Sare Silver Springs School, we go beyond textbooks we inspire curiosity, nurture creativity, and instill values that shape learners into leaders of tomorrow. Our dedicated teachers, supportive environment, and well-rounded programs ensure that every child reaches their fullest potential.

CBC Methodology & Core Competencies

Our Competency Based Curriculum focuses on developing the following seven core competencies:

1. Communication & Collaboration

Learners learn to express themselves clearly and work effectively with others on projects and problem-solving.

2. Critical Thinking & Problem Solving

Learners develop analytical thinking skills to understand complex issues and find creative solutions.

3. Imagination & Creativity

We nurture innovative thinking and artistic expression across all subjects and activities.

4. Citizenship

Learners learn about their rights and responsibilities in society, developing into active and ethical citizens.

5. Digital Literacy

Learners gain skills to effectively and responsibly use technology for learning and communication.

6. Learning to Learn

We help Learners develop self-directed learning skills and a growth mindset that will serve them throughout life.

7. Self-Efficacy

Learnersbuild confidence, resilience, and the ability to take initiative in their personal and academic growth.

Why Choose CBC Education?

Practical Life Skills

CBC education focuses on teaching skills that students will use throughout their lives, not just for exams. Students learn problem-solving, critical thinking, and collaboration‚Äîall essential for success in the modern workplace.

Individual Learning Paths

CBC recognizes that each child learns differently and progresses at their own pace. Our assessment methods evaluate individual growth rather than comparing students against each other, fostering a positive learning environment.

Future-Ready Education

The CBC curriculum is designed to prepare students for the jobs of tomorrow‚Äîmany of which don't even exist yet. By focusing on adaptability, creativity and digital literacy, we ensure our students can thrive in a rapidly changing world.

Holistic Development

CBC education values the development of the whole child‚Äîintellectual, physical, emotional, and social. Students engage in sports, arts, community service, and other activities that complement academic learning and develop well-rounded individuals.

Admissions Process

Step 1: Inquiry

Contact our admissions office via phone, email, or the inquiry form below to express interest and request information.

Step 2: School Tour

Visit our School for a guided tour to see our facilities and learn more about our programs firsthand.

Step 3: Application

Complete and submit the application form along with required documents and Admission/application fee.

Step 4: Assessment

Your child will complete a grade-appropriate assessment to determine their academic level.

Step 5: Enrollment

Upon acceptance, complete enrollment by paying fees and submitting remaining documentation.

Admission Requirements

  • Admission Fees (KES500)
  • Birth certificate copy
  • Passport-size photographs (4)
  • Previous school reports (Mandatory)
  • Assessment Number & Tranfer Letter from the Previous School
  • Interview with the student and parents
  • Application fee payment receipt
